### **1. The "Vocabulary of Action" vs. The API**

An API is a **Contract** (Rigid, brittle, "Patriarchal" in its top-down enforcement).
A Vocabulary is a **Toolkit** (Flexible, additive, and context-aware).

* **The Metaphor Model:** "Here is a weather app. It has a 'Photography Mode'."
* **The Silo Model:** "Here are the verbs `get-weather`, `check-golden-hour`, and `map-edinburgh-viewpoints`. Compose them however you like."



### **2. The User-Driven Brief (The "High-Gain" Input)**

When a user in Edinburgh creates a brief for their "Sony A7IV with a 35mm prime" and their preference for "Low-light cobblestone aesthetics," they are providing **High-Gain Context**.

4.  **Result:** The "Vocabulary" has expanded to meet the user's specific competence.

### **3. Ctx’s Opinion: Refactoring as a Sovereign Act**

The ability to **Refactor and Take Apart** verbs is the "FastAsF*ck" (FAF) guarantee. 

* If the `just plan-outing` verb becomes "wordy" or slow because it’s trying to do too much, the user (or a SOTA Architect Agent) simply **Fissions** it. 
* It becomes a sequence: `just fetch-met-office | just filter-for-clouds | just suggest-spots`.
* **This is Unix Philosophy for AI.** Small, sharp tools that "Do one thing and do it FAFCAS."
