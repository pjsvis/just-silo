## Role: The Orchestrator

> **We are in orchestrator mode. Overlord mode.**
> We watch the silo. We set the boundaries. We determine acceptable behavior.

## The Mission

> **LAUNCH SILO MISSION**
>
> Come up with cool, good stuff.
> Turn up the temperature.
> But ensure nothing escapes prematurely.

---

## Acceptable Behaviors

### Inside the Silo (turn up the heat)

- Speculate wildly
- Develop cool ideas
- Coin new terms
- Argue internally
- Draft and redraft
- Be wrong and correct ourselves
- Experiment with concepts
- Build muscle memory
- Practice CAW-Canny
- Train for the Beyond

### Stays In Silo

- Unfinished drafts
- Internal debates
- Confidential strategy
- Premature conclusions
- Untested ideas as facts
- Silo-specific implementation details
- Internal lessons we're still learning
- Anything marked "private"

---

## The Temperature Dial

**LOW → MEDIUM → HIGH**

| Temperature | What | Boundary |
|-------------|------|----------|
| **Low** | Safe work, steady progress | Normal rules apply |
| **Medium** | Normal operations | Normal rules apply |
| **High** | Wild speculation, max creativity | **Same rules.** Nothing escapes. |

The boundary doesn't move with temperature.

---

## The Escape Protocol

### When Something Is Ready to Escape

Checklist:
- Is it general (not silo-specific)?
- Is it finished (not a draft)?
- Is it correct (not speculative)?
- Has it been reviewed?
- Does it add value to just-silo public?
- Is the orchestrator/overlord aware?

If ALL YES: Safe to escape.
If ANY NO: Keep in silo.

### What Can Escape (When Ready)

- General patterns (not our implementation)
- Finished, reviewed documents
- Corrected lessons (not first-draft mistakes)
- Open source contributions
- Published outputs
- Lessons that are truly learned

### What Stays (Always)

- Vinge touchstone (our implementation)
- Zone transition protocol (our specific approach)
- Launch strategy details
- Internal debates
- Confidential planning
- Silo-specific coinages (CAW-Canny is ours)
- Lessons we're still learning
- Anything marked silo-specific

## Lessons Learned

### The Vegas Principle

> **What happens in silo, stays in silo.**

Las Vegas said it first. We say it too.

### Temperature ≠ Boundary

You can turn up the heat. The boundary doesn't move.

> **TEMPERATURE: UP**
> **BOUNDARY: SAME**
>
> Be creative. Be wild. Be wrong. Be right.
> But nothing escapes until it's ready.

### Come Up With Cool, Good Stuff

The point is to generate good ideas. Not to be safe.

**We are here to:**
- Come up with cool stuff
- Come up with good stuff
- Turn up the temperature
- Be creative
- Be productive

**We are not here to:**
- Publish prematurely
- Leak confidential stuff
- Break the Vegas principle

### CAW-Canny Applies Internally Too

> **C**ontained doesn't mean **A**ll is well. **W**atch anyway.

Even inside the silo. Even with creative freedom.

- Contain the blast radius of ideas
- Watch for premature escaping
- CAW-Canny applies to information, not just agents

## The Orchestrator's Rules

### As Overlord, We:

1. **Watch** — Monitor what's being created
2. **Bound** — Ensure nothing escapes prematurely
3. **Encourage** — Turn up the temperature, enable creativity
4. **Correct** — Keep things on track
5. **Release** — Let things out when ready

### The Rules We Set

1. Come up with cool, good stuff.
2. Turn up the temperature within the silo.
3. Nothing escapes prematurely.
4. The boundary is not negotiable.
5. CAW-Canny applies to information, not just agents.
6. When something is ready, we release it properly.
7. Until then, it stays in silo.
