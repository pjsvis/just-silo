# Brief: AI Agent Workflow Consulting — Services and Positioning

## WHAT
A consulting services brief covering what I offer, how I work, who it's for, and how to engage me. Written in the same voice as the bestiary — honest, practical, process-first.

## WHAT I DO NOT OFFER
This is not a brief about general AI strategy. Not about which model to buy. Not about "implementing AI" in the abstract. Not a technology pitch.

## WHAT I DO OFFER
AI agent workflow consulting for organisations that are spending on AI tools without a clear picture of what they're getting for their money.

## THE PROBLEM I SOLVE

Every organisation I've seen adopting AI agents has the same experience:

- The tooling works in demos
- The first few tasks go well
- Costs start accumulating without measurement
- Agent autonomy grows faster than governance
- Nobody can explain why the bill is what it is
- The pipeline that worked in dev breaks in production
- Someone eventually asks "is this actually worth it?"

Nobody can answer that last question because there's no data.

## MY APPROACH

**Brief first. Evidence second. Action third.**

Every engagement starts with a brief that defines: what the work is, what success looks like, what it will cost, and what the output should be. No brief, no work.

**The cost ledger is the foundation.**

I build cost tracking into the workflow before anything else. Without a ledger, there's no feedback loop. Without a feedback loop, there's no learning. Without learning, there's just spend.

**Governance before autonomy.**

I do not deploy autonomous agents without confirming: output persistence, hard cost caps, kill conditions, and rate limiting. The Ops-Before-Governance Temptation is the most common failure I see in production deployments. I stop it before it starts.

**Tiered substrate.**

I audit the model tier for every workflow step. Using Claude-3.7 Sonnet to read receipts is not a technical problem — it's a cost governance failure. I fix it.

## SERVICES

### 1. Workflow Audit
**For:** Teams running AI agents without a clear picture of cost or quality.  
**What:** Map the current workflow. Instrument cost tracking. Identify failure modes from the bestiary. Quantify the gap between spend and output.  
**Deliverable:** A brief with findings, named failure modes, and a prioritised improvement list.  
**Duration:** 2-3 days.  
**Outcome:** You know what you're spending, why, and what to fix first.

### 2. Ops-Mode Design and Governance
**For:** Teams ready to run AI workflows autonomously (cron, file triggers, event-driven).  
**What:** Design the pipeline with governance baked in. Output persistence, rate limiting, back-pressure, escalation paths. Hard kill conditions. Model tiering by task.  
**Deliverable:** A brief defining the ops-mode architecture, the governance model, the cost envelope, and the escalation protocol.  
**Duration:** 5-10 days depending on complexity.  
**Outcome:** A production-ready workflow that runs within a defined cost envelope with visible governance.

### 3. Bestiary Workshop
**For:** Teams that want to understand AI workflow failure modes without a full engagement.  
**What:** Walk through the 18 named tendencies. Which ones apply to your workflow? What does each one look like in your context? What are the quick wins?  
**Deliverable:** A named-and-mapped failure mode map for your organisation.  
**Duration:** Half-day.  
**Outcome:** Shared vocabulary for the failure modes. Naming them is the first step to catching them.

### 4. Ongoing Retainer
**For:** Teams running ops-mode workflows that need regular review.  
**What:** Weekly cost ledger review. Failure mode debriefs. Governance adjustments. Ops-mode iterations.  
**Deliverable:** Weekly brief with: spend vs. estimate, named failure modes caught, adjustments made.  
**Duration:** Monthly minimum.  
**Outcome:** Continuous improvement of the cost/quality ratio. No surprises on the bill.

## WHAT MAKES THIS DIFFERENT

Most AI consulting is technology-first. "Here's what the models can do. Here's how to use them."

I am cost-first. "Here's what you're spending. Here's what you're getting. Here is whether it's worth it."

The cost ledger is not an accounting exercise. It is a learning system. Every entry is a data point. Every debrief is a hypothesis tested. Over time, the workflow improves — cost per output decreases, quality increases, failure modes are caught before they become expensive.

The bestiary is the diagnostic tool. The cost ledger is the measurement system. The brief is the language. Together they form an evidence-based approach to AI workflow management that most organisations do not have and do not know they need.
