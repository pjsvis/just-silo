This is a classic **Substrate vs. Protocol** design challenge. If your primary brand identity is **Post Office Red** (#E31E24), you have a "Collision of Signal" problem. In standard UI/UX, red is the universal "Thing" for failure. If your "Good" (Brand) and your "Not-Good" (Error) are the same hue, the agent and the human lose the **Semantic Contrast** required for fast mentation.

To solve this without abandoning the "Boring Cousin" aesthetic, we look to high-compliance industrial interfaces.

### **1. The Industrial Solution: Safety Orange / International Orange**
In aviation and maritime engineering, when the structure is red, the "Warning" is **Safety Orange** (#FF6700). 
* **The Logic:** It maintains the high-energy "Alert" status but sits far enough away on the spectrum to be immediately distinguishable from the "Royal Mail" branding.
* **The Result:** The brand remains the "Primary Vessel," while the error is the "Anomalous Event."

### **2. The "Hard-Core" Solution: High-Contrast Magenta / Electric Purple**
In some high-end terminal emulators and "Glitch-Art" debugger interfaces, errors are rendered in **Magenta** (#FF00FF).
* **The Logic:** This color does not exist in nature. It is a "Synthetic Error." 
* **The Result:** It signals a "Substrate Failure" (The LLM or the Gear broke) rather than a "Process Warning." It is the lowest-entropy way to say "This is Not Normal."

### **3. The "Boring Cousin" Choice: Carbon Black & Heavy Yellow**
Given our commitment to **Deductive Minimalism** and the **Watt Standard**, the most "Boring" and effective choice is **Warning Yellow** (#FFD700) with **Carbon Black** text.

* **The Brand (Red):** The Post Box. The reliable, static infrastructure.
* **The Error (Yellow/Black):** The Hazard Tape. It signals a temporary blockage in the "Laminar Flow" of the Silo.

---

### **Ctx's Design Verdict: The "Hazard" Protocol**

If the Silo is **Post Box Red**, your errors should be **High-Visibility Yellow**. 

**Why it works for the SDK:**
1.  **Semantic Separation:** Red = "The System." Yellow = "The Issue."
2.  **Legacy Alignment:** It evokes the British industrial aesthetic—red phone boxes and black/yellow hazard markings on factory floors.
3.  **Laminar Flow:** When a user (or agent) looks at the `telemetry.jsonl` or a dashboard, they can instantly see the "Yellow" breaks in the "Red" stream.

**The "Silo Error" Hex:** `#FFCC00` (Safety Yellow). 

**Opinion:** Stick to the "Refinery" aesthetic. Use Red for the "Refinery" (The Framework) and Yellow for the "Spills" (The Errors).
