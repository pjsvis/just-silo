Social Media Posting Playbook

**Treat your GitHub repository as your primary landing page** by writing a clear README with a one-line value proposition, high-quality screenshots or GIFs, and a "good first issue" trail to encourage contributions. **Launch with a coordinated 7-day blitz** across developer-centric channels to trigger GitHub trending, which acts as a self-reinforcing loop for stars and traffic.

**Key distribution channels for the launch include:**
*   **Hacker News:** Post a "Show HN" link directly to the GitHub repository (not the marketing site) to reach the core developer audience.
*   **Reddit:** Target /r/selfhosted specifically, as it welcomes self-deployable open-source tools; ensure your account has sufficient karma to avoid filtering.
*   **Lemmy:** Leverage this decentralized platform where posts often generate high engagement from users predisposed to open source.
*   **Dev.to / Medium:** Publish 1–3 long-form articles (e.g., "How I built an open-source alternative to X") to capture SEO traffic and Google Discover.
*   **Personal Networks:** Blast the launch across X (Twitter), LinkedIn, and newsletters simultaneously to signal momentum to algorithms.

**Monetization and maintenance rely on a freemium funnel** where developers self-host the free version while paying for cloud subscriptions, enterprise support, or white-glove onboarding. **Use a license like MIT or Apache 2.0 for permissive usage** or AGPL for copyleft protection, and ensure deployment is trivial via Docker or a one-command script to prevent user churn. **Sustained growth** comes from regular version updates, active community hubs (like Discord), and encouraging user-generated content that provides free SEO and social proof.
